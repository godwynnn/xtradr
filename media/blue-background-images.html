Blue is a colour that is often found in nature, for example, the pale blue colour of the day sky or the deep blue water. This is why people often describe the colour as blue and calm. However, as a cool colour, blue can sometimes look icy, distant or even cold.

Blue is closely associated with harmony and peace. It is a colour that inspires confidence, honesty, loyalty and confidence. It reduces stress and creates a sense of calm and tranquility. This is the colour of trust and responsibility.

According to Color Wheel Pro, “light blue is associated with health, healing, serenity, understanding and gentleness”. Dark blue symbolizes knowledge, strength, integrity and seriousness. Also, blue symbolizes faith, truth and wisdom.

The Psychology of the Color Blue

  • Blue is described as a favourite colour by many people and is the most preferred colour for men.
  • Since many people prefer blue, it is often perceived as a colour without threat, which may seem conservative and traditional.
  • Blue reminds you of a sense of calm or tranquility. It is often called peaceful, secure, safe and neat.
  • Blue is often seen as a sign of stability and reliability. Companies that want to create a image of security often use blue in their advertising and marketing efforts.
  • Blue can also create a feeling of sadness or estrangement. Think about how a picture that is very different from blue, for example, pictures created by Picasso during the “blue period” may seem so lonely, sad or desperate.
  • Blue is often used to decorate offices because research shows that people are more productive in blue rooms.
  • Blue is one of the most popular colours, but it is one of the least appetizing. Some weight loss plans even recommend eating a blue plate. Blue is rarely found in food, except blueberries and some plums.
  • Blue can also pulse heart rate and body temperature.
